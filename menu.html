Appetizers:

Traditional South & Latin American starter dishes, cultivated and made fresh daily.

Empanadas de Espinaca.
$5.15

In English, this dish is known as spinach and ricotta empanadas, and it is the standard pastry, filled with filling: in this case, the filling is creamy ricotta cheese, and spinach.

Bunuelos Colombianos
$4.50

Also known as Colombian cheese fritters, they are sweet-savory donuts among Latin America, filled with cottage, farmers cheese, or traditional Colombian soft fresh white cheese (queso costena) as well as tapioca starch.

Tortas Fritas
$5.00

Also known as Argentinian and Uruguayan fry bread, they are fried biscuits, containing a higher fat content. Served with house made dulce de leche (jam).

Yuca Frita
$4.75

Also known as South American yuca fries, they are made from yuca roots of the cassava vegetable They are prepared just as french fries are, with the option for them to be boiled, mashed, or fried.

Fainá
$3.50

Also known as garbanzo flatbread, it is a nutty and peppery flatbread, and made from garbanzo flour.

Pupusas
$5.00

Also known as stuffed corn tortillas, the tortilla part is, as said, made of corn, while the inside is stuffed with cheese (queso), and black beans.

Entrees:

Authentic South American entrees, cultured from fresh plant-based, or derived, ingredients.

($) Prices range from $12.50 to $15.00

Tallarines Verdes
$14.50

Also known as a South American green noodles, this green pasta dish is made of green pasta sauce (similar to pesto), topped with spinach and basil. Queso fresco cheese is also spread across the top, and in the sauce.

Ceviche de Verduras
$12.00

Also known as vegetarian ceviche, it is a salad composed of mozzarella cheese, marinated onions, hot chile peppers, Andean choclo corn, cilantro, and lettuce. Cauliflower, and chickpeas may be added upon request.

Vegan Pabellon Criollo
$15.00

A traditional beans and rice dish of Venezuela which contains black beans, rice, and freshly cooked tomatoes. Comes with slices of fried plantain (tajadas), or a fried egg on top (cabello).

Pasta con Salsa Caruso
$14.00

Also known as Uruguayan pasta with Caruso sauce, it is a Uruguayan dish with thick, creamy, native sauce, mushrooms, basil, and cheese sauce. Other leafy greens made be added upon request.

Sándwich Triple
$12.50

Also known as Peruvian Triple Sandwich, it is a dish containing layers of fresh avocados, egg salad, and sliced tomatoes; it is encased in an extra layer of white bread.

Venezuelan Perico
$13.50

This dish is the Venezuelan version of scrambled eggs, but with the addition of onions and tomatoes, as well as peppers. Traditionally, it is served inside arepas, which is a Venezuelan and Colombian corn cake.
